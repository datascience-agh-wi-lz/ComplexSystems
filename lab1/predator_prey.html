Extending the Predator-Prey Model in Economics¶

To create a more detailed predator-prey model in economics with at least three dynamic variables ((D \geq 3)), we can incorporate the following elements:

  1. Market Capital (K): A dynamic variable representing the financial and capital resources of firms. The capital of large firms (predators) increases as they take over smaller firms, while the capital of small firms (prey) decreases due to competition.
  2. Market Share (M): A variable describing the firms' share of the market. Large firms (predators) increase their market share at the expense of smaller firms, while the market share of small firms decreases.
  3. Investment Strategy (I): A variable representing the firms' investment strategies, affecting how quickly they can grow their capital and market share. Large firms may speed up their investments to outcompete, while small firms may need to scale back their investments.

Formalization of the Model¶

Explanation of the Model¶

Predators' Capital Growth (𝐾):

This equation shows that predators' capital grows based on its current amount, the effectiveness of their investment strategies, and is negatively impacted by competition from prey firms' market share.

Prey Firms' Market Share (𝑀):

This equation indicates that the market share of prey firms can grow independently but is negatively affected by competition with predators. However, it can also benefit from any strategic investments made by the predators.

Predators' Investment Strategy (𝐼):

This equation captures the growth of the investment strategy for predators, showing it can increase or decrease based on their current strategy effectiveness, the competition posed by prey, and the capital-to-market share ratio.

Conclusion¶

These reformulated equations create a more cohesive model where the investment strategy is a dynamic variable that interacts meaningfully with both capital growth and market share. This enhances the model's realism, better reflecting the competitive dynamics between large and small firms in an economic context.

import numpy as np
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
from scipy.integrate import odeint

# Parameters
alpha = 0.1    # Growth rate of predator's capital
beta = 0.02    # Impact of prey's market share on predator's capital
theta = 0.05   # Effectiveness of investment strategy on capital growth

delta = 0.1    # Growth rate of prey's market share
gamma = 0.01   # Impact of predator's capital on prey's market share
zeta = 0.03    # Impact of predator's investment strategy on market share

lambda_invest = 0.1  # Growth rate of predator's investment strategy
mu = 0.01           # Negative impact of prey's investment on predator's capital
nu = 0.02           # Interaction effect between predator's capital and prey's market share
xi = 0.01           # Effect of market share loss on predator's investment

# Define the system of equations
def model(y, t):
    K_pred, M_prey, I_pred = y
    dK_pred_dt = alpha * K_pred + theta * I_pred - beta * K_pred * M_prey
    dM_prey_dt = delta * M_prey - gamma * M_prey * K_pred + zeta * I_pred
    dI_pred_dt = lambda_invest * I_pred - mu * M_prey + nu * (K_pred - M_prey) + xi * M_prey
    return [dK_pred_dt, dM_prey_dt, dI_pred_dt]

# Initial conditions
K_pred0 = 10  # Initial capital of predators
M_prey0 = 5   # Initial market share of prey
I_pred0 = 2   # Initial investment strategy of predators
y0 = [K_pred0, M_prey0, I_pred0]

# Time points to evaluate the solution
t = np.linspace(0, 100, 3000)  # From t=0 to t=100

# Solve the ODE
solution = odeint(model, y0, t)

# Plotting the results
K_pred, M_prey, I_pred = solution.T

plt.figure(figsize=(12, 8))

plt.subplot(3, 1, 1)
plt.plot(t, K_pred, label='Predators Capital (K)')
plt.title('Predators Capital Over Time')
plt.xlabel('Time')
plt.ylabel('Capital')
plt.grid()
plt.legend()

plt.subplot(3, 1, 2)
plt.plot(t, M_prey, label='Prey Market Share (M)', color='orange')
plt.title('Prey Market Share Over Time')
plt.xlabel('Time')
plt.ylabel('Market Share')
plt.grid()
plt.legend()

plt.subplot(3, 1, 3)
plt.plot(t, I_pred, label='Predators Investment Strategy (I)', color='green')
plt.title('Predators Investment Strategy Over Time')
plt.xlabel('Time')
plt.ylabel('Investment Strategy')
plt.grid()
plt.legend()

plt.tight_layout()
plt.show()
